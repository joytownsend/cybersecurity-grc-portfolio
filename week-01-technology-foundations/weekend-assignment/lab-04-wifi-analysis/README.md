## Comparison of WiFi Frequency Bands

### 2.4 GHz

Range  
Long range and strong signal through walls and obstacles.

Speed  
Slower speeds compared to higher frequency bands.

Interference  
Higher interference because many household devices operate on this band, including Bluetooth devices, microwaves, and smart home devices.

Common Uses  
Devices that require longer range but do not require high speed connections.

Examples include smart home devices, printers, and older laptops.

### 5 GHz

Range  
Shorter range than 2.4 GHz but still effective within most homes and offices.

Speed  
Faster speeds compared to the 2.4 GHz band.

Interference  
Lower interference because fewer devices operate on this frequency.

Common Uses  
Devices that require faster internet speeds.

Examples include laptops, smartphones, streaming devices, and gaming consoles.

### 6 GHz

Range  
Shortest range among the three bands.

Speed  
Very high speed and low latency.

Interference  
Very low interference because this band is newer and fewer devices currently operate on it.

Common Uses  
Modern devices that support WiFi 6E and require high performance wireless connections.

## Explanation

WiFi networks operate on different frequency bands that affect signal range, network speed, and interference levels.

Lower frequency bands such as 2.4 GHz travel farther and penetrate walls more easily, but they provide slower speeds and experience more interference from other household devices.

Higher frequency bands such as 5 GHz and 6 GHz provide faster speeds and lower latency but have shorter range and weaker signal penetration.

In this analysis, the device is connected to the 5 GHz band. This band provides faster speeds and reduced interference compared to 2.4 GHz, making it suitable for modern devices such as laptops, smartphones, and streaming devices.
